Zimbabwe

Harare is the capital and largest city of Zimbabwe, serving as the country's vibrant commercial, administrative, and cultural heart. Located in the northeastern part of the country on the highveld plateau, it is celebrated for its pleasant, subtropical highland climate. The city is famously nicknamed the "Sunshine City" due to its excellent year-round weather and the brilliant purple jacaranda trees that bloom across its avenues every October. Harare features a mix of modern high-rises, historic colonial architecture, bustling street markets, and expansive green spaces like the National Botanical Gardens. As a major economic hub, it drives the nation’s trade in tobacco, maize, cotton, and minerals, while anchoring Zimbabwe's contemporary art scene.
